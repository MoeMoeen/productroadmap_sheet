# 4. Critical modeling distinction

This is the key correction.

We must separate three different concepts:

## A. Restaurant total business

What the restaurant does overall, regardless of Qlub origin.

Examples:

* total restaurant traffic
* total restaurant transactions
* total restaurant GMV

## B. Qlub-channeled diner demand

Customers that Qlub helped bring to restaurants through discovery, incentives, loyalty, referrals, etc.

Examples:

* Qlub discovery traffic
* Qlub-channels raw traffic
* Qlub-channeled diners
* Qlub-channeled GMV

## C. Qlub-processed payment volume

GMV that goes through Qlub payment rails, regardless of whether Qlub generated the diner.

Examples:

* total restaurant GMV
* adoption rate
* Qlub processed GMV

This is essential because:

> Qlub may generate revenue from a restaurant even if it generated zero diners for that restaurant, as long as some portion of the restaurant’s transactions is processed via Qlub.

# 5. Corrected canonical KPI logic

## Top-level formula

### Qlub Revenue

**Qlub Revenue = Qlub Processed GMV × Qlub Monetization Rate**

---

## Qlub Processed GMV

**Qlub Processed GMV = Total Restaurant GMV × Net Adoption Rate**

Where:

* **Total Restaurant GMV = Active Restaurants × Average Restaurant GMV per Month**
* **Net Adoption Rate** = share of restaurant GMV processed through Qlub

---

## Restaurant GMV decomposition

**Average Restaurant GMV per Month = Restaurant Transactions per Month × Average Order Value**

**Restaurant Transactions per Month = Total Restaurant Traffic per Month × Restaurant Traffic-to-Transaction Conversion Rate × Fulfillment Rate**

This is restaurant-level total business, not necessarily generated by Qlub.

---

## Qlub-channeled diner decomposition

Now the separate Qlub demand-generation layer:

**Monthly Diners Channeled via Qlub = Active Restaurants × Average Qlub-Channeled Diners per Restaurant per Day × Days per Month**

We will use your desired assumption:

* **Average Qlub-channeled diners per restaurant per day = 10**

So:

**Monthly Diners Channeled via Qlub = 1,200 × 10 × 30 = 360,000 per month**

This becomes the baseline for the strategic KPI:
**monthly_diners_channeled_via_qlub = 360,000**

---

## Qlub discovery funnel

To model the upstream funnel:

**Qlub-Channeled Diners = Qlub Discovery Traffic × Discovery Conversion Rate**

So if conversion is 60%, and diners channeled are 360,000/month:

**Qlub Discovery Traffic = 360,000 / 0.60 = 600,000 per month**

---

## Qlub discovery traffic composition

**Qlub Discovery Traffic = Organic Qlub Discovery Traffic + Triggered Qlub Discovery Traffic**

### Organic Qlub Discovery Traffic

**Organic Qlub Discovery Traffic = Organic New Users + Organic Returning Users**

### Organic Returning Users

**Organic Returning Users = Repeat Rate × Existing Qlub Customer Base**

### Triggered Qlub Discovery Traffic

**Triggered Qlub Discovery Traffic = Incentive-driven + Social-driven + Word-of-Mouth-driven + Marketing-driven + Loyalty-driven discovery traffic**

---

## Adoption logic

**Net Adoption Rate = f(Gross Adoption Rate, Checkout Completion Rate)**

Interpretation:

* **Gross Adoption Rate** = share of restaurant transactions attempting to use Qlub
* **Checkout Completion Rate** = success/completion rate once Qlub checkout is initiated
* **Net Adoption Rate** = effective processed share of total restaurant GMV

---

## Monetization logic

**Qlub Monetization Rate = f(Relative Bargaining Power, Commercial Terms, New Monetization Models)**

---

## C. Restaurant-side total business assumptions

Now separate from Qlub-generated diners:

### Total restaurant traffic per restaurant per day

Let us assume each active restaurant gets on average:

* **100 total diner visits per day**

Then per month:

* **Total restaurant traffic per restaurant per month = 3,000**

Across 1,200 restaurants:

* **Total restaurant traffic across network per month = 3,600,000**

So now the Qlub-channeled share is:

* **360,000 / 3,600,000 = 10% of total restaurant traffic**

That is realistic and clean:
Qlub influences part of diner acquisition, not all of it.

---

## D. Transaction / basket assumptions

### Restaurant traffic-to-transaction conversion rate

Let us assume:

* **Restaurant Traffic-to-Transaction Conversion Rate = 0.75**

### Fulfillment rate

* **Fulfillment Rate = 0.95**

So per restaurant per month:

* Traffic = 3,000
* Transactions = 3,000 × 0.75 × 0.95
* **Restaurant transactions per month = 2,137.5**

### Average order value

* **Average Order Value = 42 monetary units**

So:

* **Average Restaurant GMV per Month = 2,137.5 × 42 = 89,775**

Across all restaurants:

* **Total Restaurant GMV per Month = 1,200 × 89,775 = 107,730,000**

---

## E. Adoption assumptions

* **Net Adoption Rate = 0.67**

So:

* **Qlub Processed GMV per Month = 107,730,000 × 0.67 = 72,179,100**

# 8. Corrected interpretation of key KPIs

Money Qlub captures from processed GMV.

The portion of total restaurant GMV processed through Qlub payment rails.

All restaurant GMV in the active Qlub network, regardless of customer origin.

Actual diners/customers who went to restaurants through Qlub discovery/growth channels.

The effective share of restaurant GMV that ends up being processed by Qlub.
